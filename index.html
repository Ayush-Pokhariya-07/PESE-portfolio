Body Language & Delivery Techniques

In these sessions, I learned how important our body language is during any presentation or conversation. I try to keep a straight posture, make eye contact (even with the camera when recording), and use hand movements to explain things better. I also practiced speaking clearly, changing my tone and speed when needed, and taking small pauses so people can understand my points better. Others can learn how to feel more confident while speaking and how small things like posture and voice can make a big difference.

Presentation Skills & Best Practices

in this session i learned about how to give an effective presentation like instead of just telling the facts or speaking like a robot we can be much expressive like with the help of body language, eye contact, facial expressions, content clarity and storytelling etc. I storyboard with hook, agenda, core content, and takeaway, rehearse with timing, record delivery, and use minimal transitions for clarity.

Effective Writing (Email & Resume)

During this session we learned about writing effective mails, resume etc. I follow “PIE” for emails (Purpose, Information, End) and STAR for resumes (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to drive clear action and highlight impact. S – Situation: What was the task or problem? T – Task: What was my responsibility? A – Action: What steps did I take? R – Result: What was the outcome or success?

Group Discussion Dynamics & Roles

In GD practice, I tried different roles like being the moderator, giving ideas, and summarizing the discussion. I learned how to listen properly, respect others’ opinions, and speak up at the right time. I also try to bring the group back on track if we go off-topic. Others can learn how to work better in a team, speak with confidence, and make sure everyone’s voice is heard. I rotate as moderator, contributor, and summarizer—setting ground rules, paraphrasing, and summarizing to foster inclusive, goal-oriented discussion.
